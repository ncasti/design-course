Lesson 5: Prevent user error

Use force functions to decrease the chances users will make mistakes. Allow users to revert potentially disruptive actions.

Mistakes are inevitable, so a well-designed system is forgiving and designed to accommodate user error.

User errors can happen for a variety of reasons. Slips occur when the usersâ€™ intentions are appropriate, but they become distracted and do something different than they intended. For instance, they might add salt instead of sugar to coffee. Mistakes occur when the user's intentions are inappropriate given the task at hand. This may be due to inconsistencies between the design and the user's interpretation, such as pulling a door that's meant to be pushed.

Good designs anticipate user error and take steps to prevent them while keeping the UI simple and instruction-free.
