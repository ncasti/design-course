Exception to the rule: Hidden affordances

Affordances do not always need to be explicit. Hidden affordances are design features that allow for certain interactions, but are not immediately obvious or apparent to users. For example, words with hints on Duolingo are interactive, yet we simply use a gray, dotted line to denote that hints are available.

Hidden affordances may come in handy when trying to maintain a clean or minimal aesthetic, or when you want to avoid drawing too much attention to an object. In our lessons, for example, we didn't want learners to over-rely on hints.
